00. Foundations

The quantum ideas behind all five

Every application below leans on the same handful of quantum concepts. Here's the short version before we get into finance.

Qb

Qubits

The basic unit. Unlike a classical bit stuck at 0 or 1, a qubit's state is a mix of both until measured.

Sp

Superposition

A qubit can hold a blend of 0 and 1 at once, so a circuit can represent many possibilities at the same time.

En

Entanglement

Correlating qubits lets a circuit capture relationships, like how two assets move together.

Fm

Feature maps

Real numbers, like prices, get encoded into qubit rotation angles so a circuit can act on them.

Vc

Variational circuits

A circuit with tunable knobs, trained the way a classical neural network is trained.

Pe

Phase estimation

Quantum Amplitude Estimation, used below in Risk and Options, is built on the same Quantum Phase Estimation subroutine that powers Shor's algorithm (Shor, 1997), the result that first proved quantum computers can beat classical ones.

01. Stock price prediction

Same data, two ways of searching it

A classical model tests patterns in price history sequentially, one hypothesis at a time. This project's quantum model is a Variational Quantum Classifier: each day's price data gets encoded into qubit rotation angles θᵢ = πxᵢ, producing a state |ψ(θ)⟩ = U(θ)|0⟩⊗ⁿ that holds every candidate pattern in superposition at once. Entangling gates between qubits let the circuit capture relationships between features, like momentum and volatility, that a classical model would otherwise have to search for separately (Schuld and Killoran, 2019).

02. Portfolio optimisation

Finding the best mix of assets

Picking the best mix of assets grows exponentially harder as more assets are added, classically it means testing combinations one by one. This project's quantum approach reframes portfolio choice as a QUBO problem: minimise wₜΣw − λμₜw, mapped onto qubits so each possible portfolio becomes a measurement outcome. QAOA (the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm) uses superposition to hold all 2ⁿ combinations at once, and entanglement between qubits encodes correlations between assets, alternating a cost step and a mixer step to steer that superposition toward the best combination (Kerenidis, Prakash and Szilágyi, 2019).

03. Risk analysis

Fewer simulations, same confidence

Estimating risk classically means running thousands of random simulations, because Monte Carlo error only shrinks as O(1/√N). Quantum Amplitude Estimation (QAE) reaches the same accuracy with O(1/N) samples instead, a quadratic speedup. QAE runs on the same Quantum Phase Estimation subroutine that powers Shor's algorithm, but instead of finding a number's factors, it estimates the probability of a "bad outcome", like a large loss (Orús, Mugel and Lizaso, 2019).

04. Option pricing

Pricing derivatives faster

Option prices are usually estimated the same way, with Monte Carlo simulation, since there's no clean formula for most real contracts. Pricing an option is really just estimating an expected payoff, so it maps directly onto Quantum Amplitude Estimation, the same algorithm used for Risk Analysis above, and inherits the same quadratic speedup: classical error shrinks as O(1/√N), quantum error shrinks as O(1/N) (Orús, Mugel and Lizaso, 2019).

05. Future of quantum finance

Where this is realistically headed

None of this beats classical methods yet on real hardware, today's qubits are too few and too noisy (the NISQ era). But the roadmap is public, and finance is one of the industries watching closest (Herman et al., 2023).

Now: NISQ era

Small, noisy quantum computers. Good for research and small demos, not yet for beating classical finance tools.

Near-term: error mitigation

Techniques to soften noise let slightly bigger problems run reliably, without needing full error correction yet.

Later: fault-tolerant quantum

Enough error-corrected qubits to run algorithms like full quantum amplitude estimation at real financial scale.

Bottom line: quantum machine learning in finance is a research bet, not a product yet. The value right now is understanding the mechanism, so when the hardware catches up, the ideas aren't a surprise. It's also worth knowing that the same phase estimation subroutine behind QAE is what makes Shor's algorithm a long-term threat to today's encryption, another reason banks are watching quantum computing closely, for opportunity and risk.

07. TL;DR

The five-line version

Stock prediction: encode price data into qubits, use superposition and entanglement to search patterns in parallel.
Portfolio optimisation: reframe as a QUBO problem, use QAOA to explore all combinations at once instead of one by one.
Risk analysis: Quantum Amplitude Estimation reaches the same accuracy as Monte Carlo with far fewer samples, O(1/N) vs O(1/√N).
Option pricing: the same QAE speedup applies, since pricing is just estimating an expected payoff.
Right now: none of this beats classical methods on real hardware yet, but the math and the roadmap are real.
