Machine learning has been used for years to try to find patterns in stock market data. But what if a quantum computer could do it differently — looking at many possibilities at once instead of one at a time?

In this project, I'm exploring how quantum machine learning (QML) could be applied to stock price prediction, using concepts like superposition and entanglement, and comparing it to a classical ML model on the same data.

Over the next few posts, I'll break down:
- What quantum computing actually is 
- How classical ML predicts stock trends today
- How quantum ML works differently — and what its real-world limitations are right now
- My results comparing the two approaches
